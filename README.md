# Statistical Intuition and Applications (UL) — Course Redesign (Summer 2025)

This repository documents the Summer 2025 redesign of **Statistical Intuition and Applications**, a first-year undergraduate course for Entrepreneurship & Innovation students. The redesign emphasizes **statistical intuition**, **frequentist reasoning**, and **business-driven decision-making** through structured, active-learning sessions.

## Target learners
- First-year undergraduates (Entrepreneurship & Innovation)
- Mixed confidence in math; highly motivated by real-world business contexts

## Design goals
- Build intuition first (data → interpretation), then formalize (notation → models).
- Minimize algebraic overhead; maximize meaning, uncertainty, and decisions.
- Use repeatable lesson structure to reduce cognitive load and improve transfer.

## Session structure (repeated pattern)
Each lesson includes:
- **Pre-Class Work (PCW)**: short prep + basic practice
- **Hook**: a business framing or short stimulus
- **Polls**: quick checks (≤5 minutes) to activate prior work
- **Breakout**: team-based application in a realistic business scenario
- **Closure**: reflection + decision justification

3. Launch Jupyter and open notebooks in `03-notebooks/`.

## Redesign summary (Summer 2025)
- Breakouts were developed from scratch and grounded in business scenarios.
- Sessions follow a consistent PCW → Polls → Breakout cycle to support applied learning.
- Notebooks and datasets were designed to be reusable and easily adapted.
